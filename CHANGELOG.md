# Changelog

## 1.2.0 — 2026-07-20

### Changed

- Replaced the article-map interview with one compact decision between two advocated article routes.
- Added private reader-awareness inference and flexible persuasive-architecture guidance before outlining.
- Created the working outline directly from the selected route, then strengthened its claims through proof before approval.
- Removed the article-map generator and updated workflow guidance, visuals, documentation, and tests around the new progression.

## 1.1.0 — 2026-07-20

### Changed

- Made premise discovery reader-first with one conversational prompt covering audience, belief shift, and present stakes.
- Added private audience-frame, worldview-fit, and language-fit analysis to every premise scout and finalist review.
- Allowed bounded audience research when project context is insufficient while preserving the writer's description as authoritative.
- Added one shared reader, belief-shift, and why-now summary before the three premise directions.

## 1.0.0 — 2026-07-19

Remarkable's first public release.

### Included

- Premise discovery with materially divergent options and capacity-aware scout delegation.
- Early objection pressure-testing and a durable `PREMISE.md`.
- Optional Personal Authority stories and practical framework design.
- Article maps, evidence plans, working outlines, and visual placeholders.
- Claim-to-proof research guidance with explicit truth boundaries.
- Full drafting, Slopless hygiene, Remarkable critique, and optional Roughdraft review.
- Compact workflow wayfinding and human approval at consequential decisions.

### Known boundaries

- Roughdraft is optional and can vary by operating system; chat and Markdown remain the fallback review surface.
- Subagent capacity differs by runtime, so premise scouts may run in waves or use the documented fallback.
- Remarkable does not invent evidence, imitate a writer's voice, or replace the writer as final editor.
