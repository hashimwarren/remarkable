# Opening

Build a reader contract, not merely a hook. Let the governing premise control the opening, but do not require the opening to disclose the article's complete answer. Establish the reader's situation, the article's promise, what makes this argument different, and why it matters now before attention dissipates.

Read [narrative-tension.md](narrative-tension.md). When the premise and approved Personal Authority create a consequential contrast, failed expectation, or unexplained result, establish that situation and make the reader want to understand it. The question may appear in the first sentence, emerge after the story, or follow the problem and stakes. Choose the placement that makes the question intelligible rather than defaulting to a question-led opening.

Ask one question when guidance is needed:

> What does the reader need to recognize immediately for this premise to feel important now?

Diagnose or draft only the opening when the user invokes this mode. Lead with meaningful change, avoid throat-clearing, and do not force a story, statistic, question, mystery, or dramatic fragment when the premise calls for a quieter entrance. Supply all context the reader needs to interpret the situation; delay only the mechanism or insight whose later resolution gives the argument forward pressure.

Check that the opening:

- creates forward pressure without manufacturing urgency;
- makes a clear promise the article can fulfill;
- is governed by the premise even when its complete answer arrives later;
- when a reveal is planned, preserves it rather than explaining the situation prematurely;
- preserves the project's voice and style; and
- creates a tension or expectation the body can resolve and the ending can complete.
