# Opening

Build a reader contract, not merely a hook. Establish the reader's situation, the article's promise, what makes this argument different, why it matters now, and the governing premise before attention dissipates.

Ask one question when guidance is needed:

> What does the reader need to recognize immediately for this premise to feel important now?

Diagnose or draft only the opening when the user invokes this mode. Lead with meaningful change, avoid throat-clearing, and do not force a story, statistic, question, or dramatic fragment when the premise calls for a quieter entrance.

Check that the opening:

- creates forward pressure without manufacturing urgency;
- makes a clear promise the article can fulfill;
- reaches the premise early;
- preserves the project's voice and style; and
- creates a tension or expectation the ending can complete.
