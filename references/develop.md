# Develop the Argument

Build every major movement through **claim, proof, and consequence**.

- **Claim:** What must the reader believe?
- **Proof:** Why should the reader believe it?
- **Consequence:** What changes because it is true?

Ask one question when guidance is needed:

> What must the reader believe before they can accept the premise, and what are the two or three essential steps that will get them there?

Reject generic claims, disconnected research, evidence dumps, and sections that merely relate to the topic. Every section must establish, prove, deepen, complicate, or apply the governing premise.

A consequence is not a recap. Translate the claim and proof into stakes, implications, decisions, risks, or action. Remove orphan claims and proof that never becomes meaningful.

Keep the framework internal in finished prose. Do not label published sections “Claim,” “Proof,” and “Consequence” unless the user's format calls for it.
